Understanding the 6-Points Based System of the Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte)

Posted by Neha Adikane on January 29, 2024

T he Opportunity Card, also known as Chancenkarte, relies on a points system. It permits foreign workers to come and work for up to 20 hours weekly while seeking a job in their field.


To be eligible for the Opportunity Card, you need at least six points out of 14. Everyone must meet specific criteria:



  • Basic German (B1 level) or Fluent English (C1).

  • Proof of financial self-sufficiency.

  • A professional qualification or degree requiring at least two years of training or study.

Even if you're not a recognized skilled worker in Germany, you can still apply if you earn enough points. The point system considers qualifications, language skills, work experience, connection to Germany, and age. Earning a minimum of 6 points qualifies you for the Opportunity Card.

Speculative Points System for the Opportunity Card:

The Opportunity Card uses a points-based system, with a total of 14 points available. Eligible, individuals must score a minimum of 6 points and it's important to note that these calculations are speculative and pending official approval.

  • Points for Academic Qualifications (Up to 4 points): Individuals with recognized foreign academic qualifications (University degrees or training) can earn up to 4 points.


  • Points for Professional Work Experience (3 points): Jobseekers with professional work experience in their chosen field can earn 3 points.


    • Points for Language Proficiency (Up to 3 points): Language proficiency in German can earn points:


  • C1 Level: 3 points

  • B2 Level: 2 points

  • B1 Level: 1 point


    • Points for Previous Stay (1 point): Individuals who have previously lived in Germany for 6 consecutive months earn 1 point.



    Points for Age (Up to 2 points):


  • Individuals under 35 years: 2 points

  • Individuals between 35 and 40 years: 1 point


Points for Spouses (1 point): Jobseekers with spouses meeting Chancenkarte requirements earn 1 point.


It may differ or change, do check the German Embassy's Official Website before Applying.


Conclusion:

This is a potential description of the system, not its current state. We'll keep you informed as new details emerge about the Chancenkarte. Also, note that, in certain situations, you might apply for an Opportunity Card while residing in Germany. However, this depends on your current visa type and meeting specified requirements.
