A Comprehensive Guide to Healthcare System for International Students in Germany 2024

Posted by Neha Adikane on April 30th 2024

H ello, future international students! Understanding the German healthcare system is crucial for a smooth study abroad experience. Germany provides universal coverage, including international students. Statutory/ Public Health Insurance (SHI) offers comprehensive services, while Private Health Insurance (PHI) provides personalized options. Your health is important; we are here to help you navigate Germany's healthcare system. Let’s get started!



Is Health Insurance Required for International Students?

Yes, health insurance is mandatory for international students in Germany. Without it, you cannot obtain a student visa or a residence permit. German law mandates healthcare coverage for all residents. German healthcare is well-regulated and known for its quality. Health insurance ensures easier access to healthcare and prevents hefty medical bills.

Visiting a doctor in Germany without insurance costs €30 to €60 for a basic consultation. While this might not sound like much, additional expenses can quickly add up, leading to unaffordable bills on a student budget. The health insurance requirement for international students in Germany should be seen as a positive aspect rather than a hassle.



Advantages of the German Healthcare System for International Students:

Comprehensive Coverage and Quality Care: International students in Germany have lifetime access to various medical services, ensuring they receive quality healthcare, including routine check-ups, specialized treatments, and emergency care.

Affordability and Co-Payments: Germany's Statutory/Public Health Insurance (SHI) offers affordable monthly premiums, tailored to students' budgets. Co-payments for medical services are generally low and manageable.

Specialized Treatments and Facilities: Students benefit from Germany's advanced medical research and technology, gaining access to specialized treatments and modern facilities.

Supportive Environment: The healthcare system prioritizes patient-centered care, fostering open communication between professionals and patients, and creating a positive experience for international students.

Health Promotion: Germany emphasizes preventive care, promoting regular check-ups, vaccinations, and screenings to maintain good health. International students can actively engage in these initiatives to enhance their well-being.

Types of Healthcare System for International Students in Germany:

Public Health Insurance for Students:


  • Students under 30, in degree courses in Germany must have public health insurance.

  • Most residents in Germany (about 90%) are registered with public health insurance.

  • Monthly premiums for students are around €110, which is lower than other situations.

  • You cannot register for public health insurance if you have insurance from your home country recognized in Germany, are over 30, are self-employed, or are not yet enrolled in a degree course.



Exemption From Public Health Insurance:


  • Students under 30 who want private health insurance must request an exemption within three months of starting their studies.

  • Once exempt, you cannot re-enroll in public health insurance until employed again.

Private Health Insurance for Students:


  • Required for students over 30 and those in preparatory or language courses.

  • Students can still opt for private insurance even if not eligible for public insurance.

  • Costs range from €100 to €300 per month, depending on factors like age and coverage.

Travel Health Insurance:


  • Travel insurance may not cover essential medical needs and could lead to challenges with visas or residency.


Understanding the German Healthcare System:


  • Germany's healthcare system is among Europe's best, covering all residents.

  • Public health insurance is state-provided and covers 90% of residents, while private insurance is offered by private companies.

  • Eligibility for private insurance depends on factors like salary, employment status, and age.

  • Public insurance is income-based, while private insurance is based on risk profile.

  • Private insurance may offer more specialized services but may cost more and require upfront payments.

What Should Student Health Insurance in Germany Include?

  • In-patient hospital care.

  • Out-patient care.

  • Regular medical check-ups.

  • The cost of medications.

  • Basic dental care.

  • Pregnancy

  • Emergency services (ambulance)

  • In case of death: Repatriation of remains

  • Funeral costs


Below are the simple steps to apply for health insurance as an international student in Germany:

Check Eligibility: Determine if you qualify for public or private health insurance. Remember, if you're over 30, public insurance may not be an option.

Select a Provider: Research different health insurance companies in Germany. Compare their offerings to find the one that fits your needs best.

Fill out the Application: Get the application form from your chosen provider's website or apply online. Make sure to provide accurate information.

Receive Certificate: If your application is approved, you will get a health insurance certificate. This is crucial for university enrollment.

Pay Premiums: After receiving the certificate, pay your insurance premiums per the provider's instructions.

Is Health Insurance Free for International Students in Germany?

No, health insurance is not free for students, but it's subsidized to make it affordable. If you are struggling financially, seek guidance from the German National Association for Student Affairs.

How Much Does Health Insurance Cost for International Students?

On average, student health insurance in Germany ranges from EUR 120 to EUR 200 per month. The cost depends on factors like the type of insurance (public or private) and your circumstances. Students generally pay lower premiums compared to other residents.


Can Students Switch Between Public and Private Health Insurance During Staying in Germany?

If you qualify for public health insurance (like being under 30 and studying for a regular degree), you might switch to private insurance after about a year. But once you switch to private, you usually cannot go back to public insurance while studying in Germany.

If you are already on private insurance, going back to public insurance is harder. It's only possible in certain situations, like if you become eligible for public insurance again (like changing your degree or turning 30).


What Health Insurance Options are available for Language Students in Germany?

For students taking language courses in Germany, whether for a semester or a year, health insurance is essential. Unlike regular degree students, language course students cannot get public health insurance. They must opt for private health insurance instead. If your language course is short, like just a semester, students from EU countries might use their EHIC card, but it's best to check with their home or German health insurer.

Health Insurance for Exchange Students in Germany:

If you are an exchange student in Germany, you will need health insurance as required by law. Typically, exchange programs last for a semester, so your EHIC card might cover you during your stay. Unlike language or preparatory courses, exchange students enrolled in degree programs can get public health insurance at a discounted student rate.


Health Insurance for PhD Students in Germany:

International PhD students living in Germany must also have health insurance. However, most PhD students are over 30 and do not qualify for discounted public healthcare for students. If employed by the university, healthcare is usually automatically arranged, and contributions are shared between the student and the university. If not employed, those under 30 can get discounted student public healthcare, while those over 30 can choose between standard public healthcare or private insurance. If visiting for less than a semester, an EHIC card might suffice.

Conclusion

Once you are in Germany, it's important to have either public or private health insurance. Here's why we suggest international students go for public health insurance:



  • Similar Costs: Whether you choose public or private insurance, the costs are nearly the same for students.

  • Flexibility: You can switch from public to private health insurance whenever you want. However, going from private to public insurance is harder and only possible in certain situations.

  • Quality Matters: Not all private insurance plans are better than public ones. Plus, good private health insurance policies can be expensive.

Healthcare in Germany is open to everyone, no matter their insurance type.
