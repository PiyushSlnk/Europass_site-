Canada Vs Germany: Choosing the Ideal Destination for Indian Students 2024

Posted by Neha Adikane on May 28th 2024

C anada and Germany stand out as leading destinations for international students, collectively hosting an average of 330,000 students from around the world. When deciding where to pursue your studies, numerous factors warrant consideration. Among the myriad options, Canada and Germany emerge as prominent choices. Each boasts distinct advantages and challenges, shaping the educational experience for international students.

Selecting the right university and study destination can be daunting. As you chart your course for studying abroad, myriad considerations come into play. Both Canada and Germany have witnessed a surge in popularity among students seeking higher education and pathways to work-study visas. As you contemplate your educational journey and career aspirations, it is essential to explore the intricacies of each country's offerings.



Germany is famous for its excellent education system. It offers affordable education, a wide range of programs, and many opportunities for work after you finish studying. Canada is known for its multicultural environment, top-notch universities, and friendly atmosphere. Whereas, Germany has a complicated bureaucracy and language differences that might be challenging to navigate. On the other hand, Canada has a higher cost of living, which could be tough on your finances.



Below are the top 6 reasons to help you decide whether Canada vs Germany is the best study-abroad destination for Indian students in 2024:

Canada:

  • To enroll in an undergraduate program in Canada, you typically need to have completed 12 years of schooling. Additionally, you will need to take the SAT exam for admission.

  • Undergraduate degree programs usually last for 4 years, while diploma courses take 2-3 years.

  • Postgraduate admissions are generally more flexible, but for management degrees, you will need to take the GMAT exam, and for engineering degrees, the GRE.

  • Canadian universities do not have strict cut-off scores; they usually consider an average score for ongoing courses.

  • Language proficiency tests like TOEFL and IELTS are mandatory for visa applications.


Germany:

  • German universities, mostly funded by the state but not regulated by it, set their eligibility criteria.

  • To pursue an undergraduate course, you often need to achieve a high score, around 90%, in your 12th board exams.

  • If you've cleared the JEE Advanced exam, the cut-off requirement may be lower, around 50%.

  • For postgraduate studies, you'll need a German equivalent of a bachelor's degree. You can also take a preparatory course called Studienkolleg for a year in your chosen field.

  • Some need proficiency in IELTS and TOEFL is necessary, along with a working knowledge of the German language.

  • Some courses may require GRE and GMAT scores.

  • For courses taught in German, proficiency tests like DSH or TestDaf are essential.

It may differ or change, do check the University’s Official Website before Applying.


2. Tuition Fees: Canada vs Germany


  • When considering where to study abroad, affordability plays a big role, and both Germany and Canada offer options that are within reach for many students. In Germany, public universities typically don't charge tuition fees for international students, which makes it a popular choice for those on a tight budget. .

  • In Canada, tuition fees for international students can be over 20,000 Canadian dollars per year, even at public universities, and vary depending on the university and program. While they are generally higher than in Germany, they tend to be more reasonable compared to other English-speaking countries like the US and UK.


3. Living Costs: Canada vs Germany

  • To cover basic expenses such as food, rent, and bills, the German government recommends international students have at least €861 per month for 12 months in a Blocked Account. In Canada, basic costs amount to around CAD 800, with an additional CAD 1080 needed for on-campus accommodation.

  • Living expenses in Germany can be relatively high, especially in big cities like Munich and Berlin. Also, the cost of living in Canada varies depending on the location, with cities like Toronto and Vancouver being more expensive than smaller towns.

  • In Canada, international students who reside for more than 183 days are required to file their taxes, regardless of whether they earn income or not. On the other hand, international students in Germany don’t need to pay taxes if their income stays below €450 per month. However, if their earnings exceed this threshold, they can file taxes and potentially receive refunds on expenses like home repairs, moving costs, and training.



4. Language Proficiency: Canada vs Germany


  • When students go to study in another country, being able to speak the language is important.

  • In Germany, even though German is the main language, a lot of people also speak English, which makes it easier for international students to get around and talk to people. Whereas, Canada, has two official languages, English and French, but most people speak English. It's good for students to know this so they can communicate better while they're there.


5. Option to Work While Studying: Canada vs Germany


  • In both Germany and Canada, international students have the chance to work while they study. In Germany, students from outside the EU can work up to 120 full days or 240 half days in a year. In Canada, you can work up to 20 hours per week during the school term and full-time during breaks. However, you need to meet the conditions set out in your study permit to do this.


Job Opportunities and Immigration Prospects:

Germany has a strong economy and a high demand for skilled workers, which is great for international students looking for jobs after they graduate. There's also a Post-Study Work Permit available, allowing graduates to stay in Germany for up to 18 months to find work related to their studies.

Canada also has a robust economy and a friendly immigration policy. There are many pathways for international students to become permanent residents, like the Canadian Experience Class and the Federal Skilled Worker Program.

Both Germany and Canada provide excellent job opportunities and immigration pathways for international students.


6. Visa Requirements: Canada vs Germany

To study in Germany or Canada, you will need a student visa. The process for getting a visa is quite straightforward in both countries, and they have similar requirements. Besides the usual visa application documents, international students must show they have enough money, an acceptance letter from a university, proof of previous education, and health insurance.


Canada:

  • Students need a study permit issued by Canadian immigration authorities to study in the country.

  • Indian applicants must undergo a mandatory medical examination.

  • The visa application fee is around $150.

  • Proof of tuition fees and living expenses for a year, approximately CAD 10,000 (INR 5,79,393), is required.

  • Post-graduation work permit applications must be made within 90 days of completing the course.

  • Spouses can work full-time if they stay for a year or longer.

  • During term time, students can work up to 20 hours per week, and up to 40 hours per week during breaks.


Germany

  • Students need a residence permit if their course extends beyond 90 days.

  • The residence permit is typically valid for 2 years, but it needs renewal if the course lasts longer.

  • Students must have 10,332 EUR (INR 8,94,582) in a German-blocked account as proof of finances.

  • During an academic year, students can work for 120 full days or 240 half days.

  • Part-time jobs related to technical industries are allowed.

Comparison: Both Germany and Canada have similar visa requirements for international students. Apart from standard documents, proof of funds and university acceptance are necessary.


The main differences lie in the specific regulations:

Canada:

  • A study permit is required.

  • Post-graduation work permit must be applied for within 90 days.

  • Work is limited to 20 hours per week during term time and 40 hours during breaks.

  • Spouses can work full-time if staying for over a year.



Germany

  • A residence permit is needed for stays over 90 days.

  • Residence permits are valid for 2 years, and renewable for longer courses.

  • Work is limited to 120 full days or 240 half days per academic year.

  • Part-time jobs in technical industries are permitted.

It may differ or change, do check the German or Canadian Embassy's Official Website before Applying.

Conclusion

In conclusion, both Germany and Canada stand out as fantastic study destinations for international students. Both come with their own set of advantages and disadvantages, so the decision boils down to what matters most to you.

Germany might be the ideal choice if you are seeking a top-tier university experience without paying tuition fees. On the other hand, Canada could be more appealing if you are interested in a longer post-study work permit. It's essential to carefully weigh all factors important to you before deciding.

Both countries provide exceptional education, diverse cultural experiences, and promising career prospects. Your choice between Germany and Canada hinges on your priorities, preferences, and career goals. Take the time to assess the factors mentioned earlier and conduct thorough research to ensure your decision aligns with your aspirations.

If a post-study work permit is your priority, Canada might be the better option. However, if you are aiming for quality education on a budget, Germany's public universities with minimal tuition fees could be more suitable. Germany has passed a new skilled immigration law, which will come into force on March 1, 2024, and promises more facilitated rules for third-country workers to visit Germany for work.
