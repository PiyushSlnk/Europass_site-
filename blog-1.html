Germany's Big Bet on India

April 16



A Look at the Surge in Investments in 2024

Posted by Neha Adikane on April 16th 2024

G ermany holds the title of India's biggest trade partner in Europe and ranks high globally for trade. Over the past decade, there has been a notable increase in economic and political ties between the two nations. India was among the earliest countries to establish diplomatic relations with Germany after World War II. Today, Germany stands as one of India's key partners both bilaterally and on the global stage.

When it comes to trade, India and Germany have seen substantial growth. In the fiscal year 2022-23, their bilateral trade reached US$ 26 billion, marking a 4.9% increase. India's exports to Germany amounted to US$ 10.1 billion, showing a rise of 2.5%, while imports from Germany reached US$ 15.9 billion, up by 6.4%.

Germany ranks as India's 12th largest trading partner and the 9th largest investor in India. From April 2000 to September 2023, Germany contributed a cumulative FDI inflow of US$ 14.37 billion. It maintains a consistent position among India's top 10-12 global partners.

In the financial year 2022-23 alone, German FDI in India amounted to US$ 547 million.
Additionally, as reported by the Confederation of Indian Industry (CII), more than 213 Indian companies are currently active in Germany. These companies engage in various sectors such as trading, manufacturing, research and development (R&D), innovation, and services, further strengthening the economic ties between the two nations.

The year 2024 witnessed a remarkable increase in investments from German companies in India, signaling a strong vote of confidence in the country's economic potential and growth prospects.

Let's look at the Surge in Investments in 2024 and explore the key highlights of this growing economic partnership between Germany and India:

Record-breaking Investment- German companies poured over €5 billion into the Indian market, setting a historic milestone in bilateral economic cooperation. This substantial investment lays the groundwork for enhanced growth and collaboration between the two nations.

Expanding Business- Presence With more than 300 German companies actively operating across various sectors in India, including automotive, energy, and technology, they play a crucial role in driving economic development and fostering innovation.

Focus on Key Sectors- Investments are strategically directed towards key sectors such as automotive, energy, and technology, highlighting India's diverse opportunities. These sectors represent significant avenues for growth and investment.

Beyond Traditional Sectors- German investments extend beyond conventional sectors into healthcare and education, fostering advancements in medical technologies and educational initiatives in India.

At present, reports indicate that around 2000 German companies are functioning in India, employing approximately 4 lakh people.
