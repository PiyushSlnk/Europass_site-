Why Study in UK

The UK is Europe's top destination for international students. With over 100 universities offering thousands of study programs, students have a wide range of options. British universities are renowned for their cutting-edge research and high-quality education. There are also many exciting career opportunities for international graduates.

Ranking

The UK is famous for its universities, which consistently rank high in international rankings, with 111 institutions making the list. Prestigious universities such as the University of Oxford, University of Cambridge, and Imperial College London consistently rank in the world's top 10.

Quality of Life

Thousands of individuals live in the UK from different regions. Regardless of where you decide to attend school, you'll experience an area with a rich history and distinctive customs.

Student Satisfaction

According to reports, International undergraduate students report high satisfaction with UK higher education, with a rate initially measured at 91%.

Employability

Graduates in the UK find good employment prospects, with full-time employees earning a median average salary of £34,963, and part-time workers earning £12,527 on average. The UK offers a Graduate Route Visa, allowing international graduates to stay and work, or search for work, for two years (three years for PhD graduates). This visa provides flexibility as graduates can work at any skill level and change jobs without needing sponsorship or a job offer initially.

Thinking about studying in the USA? The United States is a top destination for international students worldwide. Studying in the USA offers a life-changing experience with numerous new opportunities. The United States is a diverse country and is considered safe for students, especially those from India, with no need to fear racism. According to US Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), 199,812 Indian students went to the USA for higher studies in 2022. This number represents a 19% increase from the previous year. Of these students, around 37% were female. American universities welcome students from diverse backgrounds, making it a hub for global education. The institutions aim to provide a strong foundation, preparing students to compete internationally. Additionally, they strive to offer high living standards for students. There are many benefits to studying in the USA after finishing high school.
