Study in AUSTRIA

Studying in Austria offers a unique blend of rich cultural experiences and high-quality education. With its picturesque landscapes, vibrant cities, and renowned academic institutions, Austria provides an enriching environment for students worldwide.

Why Study in AUSTRIA

Austrian universities and cities offer a highly multicultural environment, making it easy for international students to adjust. Degree programs are either research-focused or include practical exercises to enhance problem-solving skills.

Ranking

Austrian universities, like the University of Vienna, University of Innsbruck, and Vienna University of Technology, rank highly globally. They excel in various fields such as humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, and engineering.


Quality of Life

Austria boasts a high quality of life with excellent healthcare, education, safety, and well-being. Its cities offer efficient public transportation and abundant recreational activities, from student services to administrative support, fostering a healthy work-life balance

Student Satisfaction

International students in Austria typically express high satisfaction with their academic and living experiences. Renowned universities, vibrant culture, and a welcoming environment contribute to their positive feedback

Employability

The number of employed people in Austria is expected to increase by 0.02 million (+0.45 percent) from 2024 to 2025, reaching 4.46 million in 2025 as per reports. Students can work up to 20 hours per week with a work permit.

German Language Proficiency Tests

  • 1 - TestDaF (Test Deutsch als Fremdsprache):

    This is one of the most commonly accepted German language proficiency tests for university admission. It consists of four sections: Reading, Listening, Writing, and Speaking. Many universities require a minimum score on TestDaF, often ranging from TDN 3 to TDN 4, depending on the program.

  • 2 -ÖSD - German Language Certificate Austria::

    ÖSD exams correspond to the levels of the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (A1-C2) and “Profile Deutsch” and are internationally recognized as proof of German language competence.
English Language Proficiency Tests-

  • 1 - TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language):

    Many programs in Austria, especially at the graduate level, require proof of English proficiency. TOEFL scores are widely accepted, with varying score requirements depending on the institution and program.

  • 2 -IELTS (International English Language Testing System):

    Similar to TOEFL, IELTS scores are also commonly accepted by Austrian universities as proof of English proficiency.

  • 3 -Pearson Test of English (PTE):

    Another option for demonstrating English proficiency, this test assesses speaking, writing, reading, and listening abilities via computer-based testing
